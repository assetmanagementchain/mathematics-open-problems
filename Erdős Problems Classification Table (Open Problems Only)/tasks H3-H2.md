# Erdős Problems — H3 → H2 Downgraded (Resolved Structurally)

> This page records Erdős problems that were historically treated as H3-level,
> but have been **structurally downgraded to H2** under IPC analysis.
>
> These problems are no longer research bottlenecks.
> Remaining work is primarily **engineering, organization, and write-up**.

---

## Criteria for Downgrade (H3 → H2)

A problem is downgraded if all of the following hold:

- Anomaly families are **finite or template-bounded**
- Information amplification **reduces degrees of freedom** with scale
- Difficulty originates from historical proof style, not structural depth

## Explicit Exclusions (Not Downgraded)

The following classes are **not** included on this page:

- Prime-dominated problems
- Additive + prime hybrid problems
- High-dimensional Ramsey problems (k → ∞)
- Self-referential or recursive covering systems

These remain H3+ or H4 and are tracked elsewhere.
